What is Bitcoin?

According to their website, Bitcoin is an innovative payment network and a new kind of money. Bitcoin uses peer-to-peer technology to operate with no central authority or banks; managing transactions and the issuing of bitcoins is carried out collectively by the network. Bitcoin is open-source; its design is public, nobody owns or controls Bitcoin and everyone can take part. Through many of its unique properties, Bitcoin allows exciting uses that could not be covered by any previous payment system.

In a book by Ricky W. Griffin called 'Organizational Behavior: Managing People and Organizations', people have a trait called 'Uncertainty Avoidance', also known as a 'Preference for Stability,' which is the extent that people will feel threatened by the unknown and prefer to in clear and unambiguous situations. People continue to invest in the dollar because it is seen as a well known, stable, currency.

This chart by charts.bitcoin.com shows Bitcoin's price volatility.

The volatility makes accepting Bitcoin as a currency a big risk for business, as the Bitcoin they receive may not be worth the same tomorrow. This makes it hard to use Bitcoin as it was meant to be used, a currency, because many business don't even accept bitcoin as a form of currency.

Bitcoin also negatively affects the environment

The digiconomist.com website recently released this graph that gets the estimated energy consumption that bitcoin mining does. This is because the more you mine, the more electricity you use. This means that generating more bitcoins literally makes bitcoin a gigantic environmental hazard. According to the data in the digiconomist site, Visa consumes less energy than Bitcoin overall, despite Visa having over 10x the amount of transactions per day as compared to bitcoin.

Does this spell the end for Bitcoin?

Not quite. A similar trend happened with the dotcom bubble in America in the late 90's, as a result of the growing popularity of the internet.

History repeates itself.

The DotComBubble is the hyperinflation of Internet-based company stocks due to its growing popularity at the time. As you can see, like Bitcoin and other cryptocurrencies in the market, the stocks of these companies peaked at Y2K then the bubble burst, making the market crashed. As of right now, the cryptocurrency market is at the same stage of a market crash, and what follows is the stage of stabalization, where the price of bitcoin would stop falling and it's value would increase at a steady rate that follows historical trends

Conclusion

Not only bitcoin, but cryptocurrencies in general have been dropping in price recently. There are many reasons for this, such as the volatily of the currencies as compared to more traditional currencies and the environmental impacts mining has on the world, as well as the rising cost of entry to begin mining. However, this does not mean that you should give up on bitcoin, because as soon as the market stabilizes, bitcoin's decentralized nature and robust security through blockchain technology would make it a fine alternative to traditional currency.
